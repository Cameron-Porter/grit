# Training for Muscle Growth: Beginner to Advanced

## Key Principles
* **Fluid Training Stages**: Beginner (0-2 yrs), Intermediate (3-6 yrs), and Advanced (7+ yrs) are continuums shaped by constraints and affordances. 
* **Beginner Phase**: Focuses on neural pattern imprinting and technique. Use 2-4 days/week, 4-6 distributed compound exercises, 2-5 sets of 5-10 reps. No RIR (Reps in Reserve); add weight when bar speed is fast. Keep exercises static for 3-4 months.
* **Intermediate Phase**: Focuses on broad exploration to map individual responses. Use 3-6 days/week, 5-15 sets per muscle/week. Experiment across all rep ranges (5-30) and exercises.
* **Advanced Phase**: Focuses on highly individualized, prioritized volume targeting weak points using high-SFR (Stimulus to Fatigue Ratio) movements. Slower gains requiring precise fatigue management. 

## Scientific Takeaways
* **RIR Accuracy (Strong Evidence)**: Advanced lifters can accurately estimate proximity to failure within ~1 rep; beginners cannot.
* **Volume Thresholds (Strong Evidence)**: Beginners achieve maximal hypertrophic responses from very low volumes due to high sensitivity.
* **Systemic Fatigue (Moderate Evidence)**: Total body Maximum Recoverable Volume (MRV) is lower than the sum of individual muscle MRVs, capping overall adaptation.
* **Mind-Muscle Connection (Expert Opinion)**: Maximizing perception of tension while minimizing external load reduces joint stress while matching hypertrophic stimulus.

## Coaching Notes
* **Cueing Limits**: Give beginners a maximum of 1-2 cues per set to prevent cognitive overload and form breakdown.
* **Hands-Off Spotting**: Do not physically guide a client's arc of motion constantly; they must imprint neural stabilization patterns themselves.
* **Form > Effort**: Reps only count if form holds. Hitting 0 RIR with degraded technique is a failed set.
* **Ignore Intermediate "Vibes"**: Intermediates lack baseline data to dictate programming based on "mind-muscle connection."
* **Patience Protocol**: Stop advanced clients from adding weight and reps in the same week to prevent fatigue spikes.

## Beginner Mistakes
* **Extremes in Rep Ranges**: Using >10 reps (burn breaks technique) or <5 reps (fear of load breaks technique).
* **"Muscle Confusion"**: Changing exercises weekly instead of mastering fundamental movement patterns for 3-4 months.
* **Overestimating RIR**: Intermediates assuming they are at 2 RIR when they have 5+ reps left.
* **Ego Lifting**: Using the term "Advanced" as a badge of honor while performing basic lifts with terrible form.
* **Premature Prioritization**: Intermediates focusing on specific body parts before developing a baseline physique.

## Advanced Insights
* **Systemic Fatigue Limitation**: You cannot train all muscle groups at MRV simultaneously without burning out.
* **Volume Distribution**: Place 1/3 to 1/2 of muscles on maintenance/MEV (~8 sets/week). Push the remaining muscles to MRV (ramping from 10-15 up to 25-35+ sets/week).
* **Progression Reality**: Accept slower progression. Aim for 2.5 lbs per week OR 1 rep every other week.
* **Exercise Selection**: Choose strictly based on personal SFR data gathered during intermediate years.
* **Active Rest**: Incorporate 1-2 phases per year (2 weeks of minimal training) to shed deep systemic fatigue.

## App Implementation Ideas
* **Goals & Readiness**: Adjust progression curves based on logged training age. Lock advanced features (custom splits, MEV/MRV sliders) until intermediate benchmarks are met.
* **Beginner UI**: Fixed whole-body routines (2-4 days/week), locked 5-10 rep range, no RIR inputs. Auto-progress load based on "easy" logs.
* **Intermediate UI**: Unlocks full exercise database and RIR logging. Prompts weekly surveys on pumps/soreness to build an exercise SFR profile.
* **Advanced UI**: Unlocks Front-Burner/Back-Burner toggles for volume redistribution, 2.5lb micro-loading, and schedules mandatory 2-week active rest blocks.
* **Data Tracking**: Log joint pain, target soreness, and pump ratings to calculate an algorithm-driven "SFR Score" for personalized optimal exercises.
