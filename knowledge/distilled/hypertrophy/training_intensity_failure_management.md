# Training Intensity & Failure Management

## Key Principles
* **Proximity to Failure (RIR):** Managing "Reps in Reserve" based on training age and exercise risk.
* **Form Integrity:** Prioritizing technical precision over absolute failure, especially for developing lifters.
* **Safety-Based Selection:** Distinguishing between high-risk compound lifts (e.g., squats) and low-risk isolation (e.g., curls) to dictate safe failure limits.
* **Mesocycle Progression:** Gradually increasing intensity (decreasing RIR) throughout a training block.
* **Beyond-Failure Techniques:** Utilizing assisted reps or cluster sets only when training age and adaptation needs warrant it.

## Scientific Takeaways
| Claim | Evidence Level | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Hypertrophy & Failure | Strong | Close-to-failure training is essential; absolute failure is not. |
| Beyond-Failure Training | Moderate | Advanced lifters may need it to surpass current adaptation thresholds. |
| Technical Breakdown | Expert Opinion | Early-career training to failure ingrains poor motor patterns. |

## Coaching Notes
* **Form Killer:** Failure is a form killer for beginners; establish consistency before intensity.
* **Fatigue Calibration:** Absolute failure carries a high systemic fatigue cost; it should be a surgical tool, not a default.
* **Risk/Reward:** Never train to failure on lifts where "dying" under the weight is possible.
* **Programming:** Intensity should escalate throughout a mesocycle, not peak immediately.

## Beginner Mistakes
* **Compound Over-reaching:** Pushing absolute failure on squats/deadlifts too early in the training career.
* **Sloppy Intensity:** Using "failure" as an excuse to ignore technical breakdown.
* **Uniform Treatment:** Failing to distinguish intensity limits between a barbell squat and a cable row.
* **Systemic Neglect:** Ignoring the cumulative fatigue cost of frequent failure training.

## Advanced Insights
* **The Buffer is for Growth:** Advanced lifters use intensity boosters (beyond-failure) in the final weeks of a mesocycle to push for peak adaptation before a scheduled deload.
* **Progression:** Start mesocycles at 2–3 RIR, progressing to 0–1 RIR as the training block nears completion.
