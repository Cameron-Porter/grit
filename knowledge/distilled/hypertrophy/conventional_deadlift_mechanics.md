# Conventional Deadlift Mechanics & Safety

## Key Principles
* **The 5-Step Setup:** 1) Midfoot under bar, 2) Grip without bending knees, 3) Drop shins to bar, 4) Lift chest/set back, 5) Drag it up.
* **Hip Hinge Biomechanics:** The deadlift is a hinge, not a squat. Hips must start higher than the knees but lower than the shoulders.
* **Intra-Abdominal Pressure (IAP):** Use the Valsalva maneuver (breathing deep into the belly, bracing 360 degrees) to stabilize the lumbar spine.
* **Latissimus Dorsi Engagement:** Keep the barbell close to the body's center of mass to reduce the moment arm on the lower back.
* **Pulling the Slack Out:** Create full-body tension against the bar before the plates leave the floor.
* **Pushing the Floor Away:** Conceptualize the initial phase as a "leg press" into the ground using the quads, rather than yanking the bar upward.

## Scientific Takeaways
* **Strong Evidence:** Maintaining a vertical bar path directly over the midfoot minimizes the moment arm on the lower back, maximizing safety and mechanical efficiency.
* **Strong Evidence:** A neutral spine under heavy axial load significantly reduces shear forces on intervertebral discs compared to pulling with lumbar flexion.
* **Strong Evidence:** The deadlift generates disproportionately high Central Nervous System (CNS) fatigue compared to machine exercises, requiring specialized volume management.
* **Moderate Evidence:** An alternate (mixed) grip prevents the barbell from rolling, allowing heavier loads, but places the supinated arm at a higher risk of biceps tendon rupture if bent.

## Coaching Notes
* **Lat Cues:** Tell clients to "squeeze oranges in your armpits" or "try to bend the bar around your shins."
* **Hip Positioning:** If a client's hips shoot up before the bar leaves the floor, their starting position was too low (they tried to squat the weight).
* **The Slack Pull:** Teach this by having the client pull up just hard enough to bend the bar and hear the plates "click" against the collar, pausing before breaking the floor.
* **Stance Width:** Have the client perform a vertical jump. Their landing footprint is generally their strongest pulling stance.

## Beginner Mistakes
* Squatting the deadlift (dropping hips too low and eliminating hamstring tension).
* Rounding the lower back (lumbar flexion) during the pull.
* Hyperextending the lower back at lockout (leaning too far backward) instead of simply squeezing the glutes.
* Letting the bar drift away from the shins, exponentially increasing lower back strain.
* Yanking the bar off the floor without bracing or pulling the slack out first.
* Bending the elbows (especially on the underhand grip) to "row" the weight.
* Bouncing the weight off the floor (touch-and-go) instead of resetting full-body tension for each rep.

## Advanced Insights
* **Sets & Reps:** Strength: 3-5 sets of 3-6 reps. Hypertrophy: 3-4 sets of 6-8 reps. Heavy deadlifts are unsuited for high-rep sets due to rapid form breakdown under fatigue.
* **Intensity:** Lift heavy but stop 1-2 reps shy of failure (1-2 RIR). Absolute, form-breaking failure carries an unacceptable risk-to-reward ratio.
* **Frequency & Recovery:** Perform 1-2 times weekly. Ensure 48-72 hours of recovery before another heavy lower-body or spinal-loading session due to massive CNS fatigue.
* **Progression:** Utilize linear periodization (+5 lbs per week) for beginners, or wave periodization for advanced lifters.
* **Grip Selection:** Use Double Overhand until grip limits performance, then switch to Mixed or Hook grip.

## App Implementation Ideas
* **Fatigue Management Logic:** If a user programs conventional deadlifts, automatically flag the subsequent 48 hours to prevent auto-scheduling heavy lower-back-loading exercises (e.g., Barbell Rows).
* **Setup UI Integration:** Trigger a non-skippable "5-Step Setup Checklist" the first time a user logs a deadlift. Include dynamic cues like "Keep the bar against your shins."
* **RIR Safety Cap:** Force a hard Reps in Reserve (RIR) cap. Prevent the user from logging a "0 RIR" set without triggering a safety warning regarding injury risk. Default progression targets to 1-2 RIR.
