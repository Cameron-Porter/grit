# Accessory Training: Traps, Rear Delts, & Forearms

## Key Principles
* **Complete Trap Development:** Targeting the upper traps (shrugs) and mid-lower traps (rows/pulls) for comprehensive back thickness and posture.
* **3D Shoulder Aesthetics:** Prioritizing rear delt isolation through transverse plane movement.
* **Forearm Specialization:** Focusing on the brachioradialis (neutral/overhand grip) and the forearm flexor group.
* **Grip Efficiency:** Utilizing lifting straps on heavy back work to remove grip limitations, allowing for dedicated, non-fatigued forearm isolation later.
* **Accessory Integration:** Strategic placement of secondary muscle groups at the end of Pull sessions to avoid interfering with primary compound strength.

## Scientific Takeaways
| Claim | Evidence Level | Notes |

## Coaching Notes
* **Trap Momentum:** Most trainees fail here by using momentum. Strict, heavy loading with a slow-eccentric phase is mandatory for growth.
* **Lower Trap Importance:** Essential for scapular stability and preventing chronic shoulder impingement/posture issues.
* **Session Ordering:** Always place forearm training last; pre-fatiguing the grip will ruin your ability to load heavy back-pulling movements.
* **Rear Delt Intensity:** Do not treat rear delts as a "warm-up." They require the same intensity and progression discipline as primary lifts.

## Beginner Mistakes
* **The "Shrug Roll":** Rolling shoulders forward during shrugs increases AC joint risk and provides zero additional hypertrophy.
* **Oversized Weights:** Using too much weight on rear delt flys, causing the traps to take over the movement.
* **Unidimensional Forearm Work:** Failing to train forearms in multiple planes (flexion, extension, pronation/supination).
* **Forearm Neglect:** Treating forearm growth as an afterthought, leading to an aesthetic imbalance compared to the biceps.

## Advanced Insights
* **Fiber Alignment:** To maximize trap recruitment, lean slightly forward during shrugs to better align the tension with the descending/ascending muscle fibers.
* **Shoulder Plane:** Keep elbows slightly higher than the wrist during rear-delt movements to stay in the transverse plane and minimize trap involvement.
