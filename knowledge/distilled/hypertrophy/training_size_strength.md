# Training for Size & Strength: Core Principles

## Key Principles
* **Mechanical Tension:** The primary driver of hypertrophy, achieved through consistent progressive overload and high-effort training.
* **Progressive Overload:** The gradual increase of stress placed on the body (weight, reps, or improved technique) to force adaptation.
* **RPE/RIR Management:** Using "Reps in Reserve" (RIR) to gauge intensity. 1-3 RIR is the optimal zone for most hypertrophy training.
* **Mesocycle Periodization:** Organizing training into 8-12 week blocks to manage volume, intensity, and fatigue.
* **Technical Integrity:** The ceiling for progression. If form breaks down, the stimulus is diluted and injury risk rises.
* **Stimulus-to-Fatigue Ratio (SFR):** Selecting exercises that provide maximum tension while minimizing unnecessary systemic or joint fatigue.

## Scientific Takeaways
| Claim | Evidence Level | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Proximity to Failure | Strong | Training at 1-3 RIR is as effective as absolute failure but reduces systemic fatigue. |
| Progressive Overload | Strong | Growth is a response to increasing mechanical tension over time. |
| Training Volume | Strong | Hypertrophy is dose-dependent on volume (sets per muscle) until diminishing returns occur. |

## Coaching Notes
* **Consistency > Optimization:** A "suboptimal" program followed religiously beats a "perfect" program followed sporadically.
* **Avoid Junk Volume:** If you finish a set with 5+ reps left in the tank, you aren't providing enough mechanical tension to trigger growth.
* **Refined Technique:** As you get stronger, your form should become more efficient and refined, not sloppier.
* **Recovery is a Skill:** Tracking RIR allows you to autoregulate intensity on days when life stress or poor sleep limits your performance.

## Beginner Mistakes
* **Muscle Confusion:** Randomizing exercises instead of mastering foundational movements.
* **Failure Obsession:** Training to absolute failure on every set of heavy compounds, leading to CNS burnout.
* **Eccentric Neglect:** Ignoring the lowering phase, which is critical for muscle damage and hypertrophy.
* **Comparison Traps:** Comparing progress to elite athletes with different training ages, recovery, and volume capacities.
* **Skipping Deloads:** Ignoring recovery weeks, leading to chronic stagnation and injury risk.

## Advanced Insights
* **The "Practical Zone":** While a spectrum of reps works, prioritize the 6–15 rep range to balance mechanical tension with metabolic stress.
* **Frequency Matters:** Train muscle groups 2-3 times per week to maximize the number of high-quality "stimulus events" per mesocycle.

## App Implementation Ideas
* **Hypertrophy Foundation Module:** Automatically generate mesocycles based on user training age and goals.
* **Progression Engine:** Require logbook confirmation of improved reps or load before suggesting weight increases.

* **Autoregulation:** Reduce intensity suggestions on days where the user reports high stress or poor sleep.
