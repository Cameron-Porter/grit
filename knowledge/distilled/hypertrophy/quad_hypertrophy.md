# Quad Hypertrophy: SFR & Joint Health Optimization

## Key Principles
* **Quad Specialization:** Prioritizing deep knee flexion as the primary driver for hypertrophy.
* **Stimulus-to-Fatigue Ratio (SFR):** Selecting movements that maximize mechanical tension on quads while minimizing lower back and systemic fatigue.
* **Joint-Centric Training:** Using joint health as the ultimate arbiter of exercise suitability; avoid movements causing chronic discomfort.
* **Technical Failure Management:** Approaching technical failure (1-2 RIR) on compounds; training to absolute failure on safe isolation work.
* **Exercise Rotation:** Systematically cycling movements (every 4–6 weeks) to prevent overuse and maintain tissue health.

## Coaching Notes
* **Upright Posture:** Use the upright torso cue in squat patterns to shift demand away from the hips/glutes and onto the quads.
* **Fatigue Management:** Lower back and CNS fatigue are the primary bottlenecks for quad growth; protect these to train harder.
* **Isolation Value:** Leg extensions are elite tools for isolating quads without systemic/back fatigue risk—do not view them as "secondary."
* **Pain vs. Burn:** If a movement creates joint pain rather than muscular fatigue, it is objectively a poor exercise for the trainee. Rotate it out.

## Beginner Mistakes
* **Hip-Dominant Squats:** Utilizing excessive forward lean, turning quad work into hip-dominant posterior chain work.
* **Toughness Bias:** Training through joint pain under the guise of "discipline," leading to chronic injury.
* **Back Squat Reliance:** Over-relying on barbell back squats even when back fatigue limits quad stimulus.
* **Neglecting Tempo:** Ignoring controlled eccentric (lowering) phases and full ROM at the knee.

## Advanced Insights
* **Anatomy-Specific SFR:** A movement is only "effective" if it fits the trainee's individual anatomy without triggering pain.
* **Systemic Ceiling:** True hypertrophy progress is often limited by systemic recovery; minimizing the load on the lower back allows for higher volume on the target quad tissue.
