# Ultimate Push Workout: Chest, Shoulders, & Triceps

## Key Principles
* **PPL Split:** High-frequency, dedicated training for chest, shoulders, and triceps.
* **Hypertrophy Focus:** Maximizing growth through mechanical tension and metabolic stress.
* **End-Range Emphasis:** Targeting muscles in both fully lengthened (stretched) and fully contracted positions.
* **Inter-set Stretching:** Utilizing rest periods for 30s static stretches to enhance muscle thickness.
* **Strategic Partials:** Utilizing "stretched" vs. "squeezed" partial ranges to isolate target tissues.
* **Anatomical Variation:** Adjusting planes of motion to target specific intramuscular segments (e.g., deltoid compartmentalization).

## Coaching Notes
* **Stability:** Prioritize a rock-solid base on compound lifts; stability directly correlates to force production.
* **Programming:** While efficiency is good, prioritize effectiveness (dedicated movements) for advanced hypertrophy.
* **Execution:** Do not sacrifice the stretch for the sake of the peak contraction; both are vital.
* **Volume Management:** Use back-off sets (e.g., Larson Press at 75% intensity) to build volume after a heavy top set.

## Beginner Mistakes
* **Lack of Control:** Yanking the bar out of the rack or using momentum/swinging on overhead movements.
* **Form Neglect:** Failing to hit required depth (elbow below shoulder) on pressing movements.
* **Tunnel Vision:** Prioritizing the "pump" over progressive overload of heavy compound loads.
* **Random Partials:** Implementing partial reps without understanding whether they are targeting the stretch or squeeze phase.

## Advanced Insights
* **The "Stick" Point:** Use targeted partials (e.g., squeeze-only press-downs) to overload the hardest point of the ROM.
* **The "Stretch" Factor:** Utilize the crossbody Y-raise or overhead extensions to access ranges of motion conventional lifts miss.
* **Mechanical Adjustments:** Use 0-30° elbow tuck for benching to align with pectoral fibers while preserving shoulder health.
