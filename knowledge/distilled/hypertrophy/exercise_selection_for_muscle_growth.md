# Exercise Selection for Muscle Growth

## Key Principles
* **Stimulus-to-Fatigue Ratio (SFR):** The core metric. Maximize muscle stimulation while minimizing systemic and joint fatigue.
* **Neurological 'Groove':** Mind-muscle connection and technical efficiency improve after the first 1–2 sets of a specific movement.
* **Exercise Staleness:** Long-term adaptation leads to diminished returns, requiring strategic rotation.
* **Functional Variation:** Use exercise selection to target different muscle regions (e.g., stretch-mediated vs. peak-contraction) within a mesocycle.
* **Opportunity Cost:** Choosing a low-SFR "novelty" movement prevents the use of a proven, higher-SFR movement.
* **Systemic Fatigue:** Excessive exercise variety within a session causes unnecessary fatigue due to repeated re-warming and equipment changes.

## Scientific Takeaways
* **Neurological Efficiency (Expert Opinion):** Technical proficiency improves within the first few sets of an exercise; premature rotation prevents this adaptation.
* **Regional Hypertrophy (Moderate Evidence):** Varied angles (incline/flat) are necessary to target different muscle fiber regions for optimal development.
* **Systemic Fatigue Load (Moderate Evidence):** High exercise rotation per session increases systemic recovery demands due to repeated setup and warm-up requirements.

## Coaching Notes
* **Avoid the "Magic Exercise" Trap:** Basic, compound movements are the foundation of growth.
* **Minimalist Advantage:** High-level lifters focus on a curated pool of 5–10 high-value exercises.
* **Joint Prioritization:** If an exercise causes chronic discomfort, rotate it out immediately, regardless of its previous SFR performance.
* **Progression Bias:** Do not rotate an exercise if weight or rep progression is still occurring.

## Beginner Mistakes
* **Excessive Variation:** Performing 10+ different exercises per muscle group per session.
* **Premature Rotation:** Switching exercises despite seeing consistent progress.
* **Ignoring the Groove:** Moving to a new exercise before the nervous system has adapted to the current motor pattern.
* **Filler Movements:** Choosing novel exercises over "gold standard" compound lifts.

## Advanced Insights
* **Volume-Dependent Selection:** * 5–7 sets/session: Use 1 exercise. 
    * 7–12 sets/session: Use 2–3 exercises.
* **High-Frequency Programming:** When training a muscle 4–5x per week, rotate exercises or rep ranges to prevent overuse while maintaining stimulus.
* **Strategic Testing:** Use deload phases to test new movements with varied grips, stances, or cadences to find new high-SFR winners.

## App Implementation Ideas
* **SFR Tracking:** Prompt users to rate their perceived stimulus vs. fatigue for each movement.
* **Lock-in Feature:** Discourage exercise rotation for movements showing consistent strength gains.
* **Staleness Alert:** Automatically flag exercises for potential rotation if strength or volume metrics plateau for 3+ weeks.
* **Volume Constraint Logic:** Restrict the number of recommended exercises based on the user's total weekly set targets.
