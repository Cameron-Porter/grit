# Nutrient Timing for Hypertrophy

## Key Principles
* **Prioritization:** Total daily macronutrient intake is the primary driver of muscle growth; nutrient timing is a secondary refinement.
* **1–2 Hour Window:** Consuming protein and carbohydrates within 1–2 hours post-workout is essential to stop muscle catabolism and initiate recovery.
* **Consistency over Optimization:** If daily calorie and protein goals are not met, focusing on precise timing provides negligible benefits.
* **Energy Availability:** Nutrient distribution should ensure sufficient fuel is available during the training window to support intensity.
* **Anti-Catabolism:** Preventing prolonged post-workout fasting is critical to maximize anabolic signaling and muscle preservation.

## Scientific Takeaways
* **Recovery Initiation:** Post-workout protein and carbohydrate intake is critical for stopping catabolism and starting muscle protein synthesis (MPS) and glycogen replenishment *(Strong Evidence)*.
* **Macronutrient Hierarchy:** Total daily macronutrient intake is the dominant factor for body composition; timing is secondary *(Strong Evidence)*.
* **Anabolic Window:** Waiting longer than 2 hours post-workout to ingest nutrients is sub-optimal for maximizing the anabolic response *(Moderate Evidence)*.

## Coaching Notes
* **Prioritize Totals:** Most clients over-complicate timing while failing their daily protein/calorie targets. Always fix the "totals" first.
* **Compliance:** If a full meal is difficult post-workout, utilize liquid nutrition (protein shake/carbs) to remove barriers to compliance.
* **Avoid Over-Optimization:** If a client's diet is already highly consistent in macros, avoid adding complex timing rules that risk burnout or diet fatigue.

## Beginner Mistakes
* Prioritizing specific supplement timing while failing to meet total daily protein intake.
* Fasting for 2+ hours post-workout, which promotes catabolic signaling.
* Ignoring intra-workout or pre-workout energy availability, leading to low training intensity.
* Relying on "wacky" supplements instead of foundational food intake.
* Eating too infrequently (e.g., 1–2 meals/day), resulting in uneven amino acid availability.

## Advanced Insights
* **Target Distribution:** Aim for at least 4 meals per day to maintain consistent amino acid availability.
* **Workout Fueling:** If performance suffers, prioritize calories in the meals immediately surrounding the training session.
* **Recovery Protocol:** Always have a plan to consume nutrients immediately post-training. If a full meal is unavailable, use a shake.

## App Implementation Ideas
* **Nutrient Timing Assistant:** Send push notifications reminding users to consume a post-workout meal/shake within a 2-hour window after logging a workout.
* **Diet Checker Algorithm:** Prioritize validating daily totals (Calories/Protein) before offering timing suggestions. If daily goals are not met, suppress timing notifications to focus the user on foundational compliance.
