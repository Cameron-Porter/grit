### A-1 — Lengthened Partials as Valid Hypertrophy Technique
**File changed:** knowledge/doctrine/exercise_selection.md (Outdated Guidance section), knowledge/doctrine/progression.md (Progression Methods toolkit)
**Change type:** Addition (exercise_selection.md qualification) + Addition (progression.md new row)
**Original statement:**
> "Except in specific strength peaking contexts, partial-range training as a deliberate hypertrophy strategy produces inferior muscle development compared to full ROM training."

**Updated statement:**
> Shortened-position partials are still classified as inferior. The prohibition now explicitly applies only to shortened-position partials. Lengthened-position partials (performed in the stretched range, added after full-ROM failure) are validated as producing near-equivalent hypertrophy to full ROM at matched volume (Strong Evidence, 2026 IJES study). Added as a new progression method row: "Lengthened-position partials — After reaching full-ROM failure; isolation exercises; intermediate/advanced."

**Reason:** Multiple sources (training_past_failure.md, rom_hypertrophy_optimization.md) with Strong Evidence distinguish shortened from lengthened partials. The categorical prohibition created a false equivalence between two mechanistically different practices.
**Supporting evidence:** training_past_failure.md, rom_hypertrophy_optimization.md, optimizing_hypertrophy_rom.md, training_past_failure.json (2026 IJES study)

### A-2 — HIT / Min-Max Protocol: 1–2 Sets to Absolute Failure
**File changed:** knowledge/doctrine/foundational_beliefs.md (R-01 section), knowledge/doctrine/progression.md (Outdated Guidance)
**Change type:** Addition (qualification appended to R-01) + Addition (exception note in Outdated Guidance)
**Original statement (R-01):**
> "Diminishing returns on stimulus begin sharply after the 5th set on any single exercise... No exercise should be prescribed more than 5 working sets within a session."

**Updated statement:**
> R-01 body unchanged. Qualification appended: "The 3–5 sets range correctly describes the standard submaximal approach. However, it implies a false floor of 3 sets being universally necessary. 1–2 sets taken to absolute failure (0 RIR) produce equivalent hypertrophy to 3–4 submaximal sets, particularly for advanced lifters (Strong Evidence). This is a valid inverse volume-intensity trade-off. The HIT/Min-Max approach is advanced-only."

**Reason:** Strong Evidence for first-set primacy and diminishing returns supports the HIT alternative. Doctrine's language implied 3 sets was a universal floor, which is incorrect.
**Supporting evidence:** hit_min_max.md, hit_min_max.json, training_intensity_sustainability_hypertrophy.md
